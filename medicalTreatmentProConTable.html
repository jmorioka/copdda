Medical Treatment: Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages of Medical Treatment Disadvantages of Medical Treatment
You have more control over the success of your treatment. It doesn't work for everyone. Even if you handle your medicines well and exercise, you could get into a bad situation or catch the flu, and that could lead to you spending a long time sick in the hospital, going to the hospital multiple times, or dying.
Medical treatment of COPD is usually less expensive than getting a lung transplant (though it does depend on your insurance.) There will still be times when you are short of breath.
The medicines you are taking aren't as bad for the rest of your body as the medicines you would have to take for transplant. You’ll still need oxygen. Though you might need a little less oxygen with the right medicine and exercise, you’ll still have to use it and carry it with you.
Helping you with medical treatment can be much easier for the people taking care of you than going through the process of helping you get a transplant. You'll still need to use inhalers and other drugs to help with other problems that COPD creates in your body.
Your independence will still be limited. Depending on how sick you are, you may still need to depend on others to shop, cook, clean, drive, etc. for you.
