COPD and Treatments for It

What is emphysema?

At the end of the bronchial tubes in your lungs, there are bunches of tiny air sacs called alveoli. Alveoli inflate and deflate when you inhale and exhale. They are elastic, kind of like tiny balloons. Alveoli are important because they are where oxygen from the air gets into your blood, and where carbon dioxide from your blood gets back out into the air.

When you have emphysema, your alveoli become less elastic. This means that air stays in them when it shouldn't. Since old air gets stuck in the alveoli, new air can't get into the alveoli, and new oxygen can't get into your blood. This makes people with emphysema short of breath, sometimes even when they aren't moving at all.
