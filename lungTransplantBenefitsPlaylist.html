Lung Transplant Surgery: Advantages

Video Transcripts

Video 1: Bill

I got a transplant 6 months ago. Before my transplant, I was so short of breath that I felt as if I was breathing through a straw. My anxiety would often get the best of me because I never knew when I would. But now, I don’t feel short of breath. I no longer have to carry an oxygen tank everywhere. I can get around the house. I can even take care of my own shopping, cooking and cleaning. Getting my lung transplant has helped me get my freedom back.

Video 2: Ken

It’s been 5 years since I’ve got my transplant. It took years before I finally got the call that there were lungs that were a good fit for me. I was one of the lucky ones. I sailed through the lung transplant surgery and went home within 2 weeks. The doctors said that it was because I went to pulmonary rehab and was so strong going into the surgery.

The first two years weren’t easy, but they weren’t awful. I had to learn a lot about how to take care of myself. I had spent a lot of time getting my labs drawn, my pulmonary function tests measured and visiting with my doctor. I had to stay overnight in the hospital several times for rejection, but I always made it out in one piece.

The best part of getting a lung transplant was that I was able to travel with my wife again. We have gone down to Florida every year for the last 3 years now. I have also gotten to know my grandchildren. I saw my oldest grandson graduate from high school and I held my youngest granddaughter just hours after she was born. Just think, I might not have met her if I hadn’t had a transplant.

I am starting to slow down now though. I have something called bronchiolitis obliterans syndrome which is called BOS for short. Although, I have had about 5 really good years, my lungs are starting to break down and I feel short of breath again. I doubt that I will make it back down to Florida this year. Even though I feel sick again, I wouldn’t trade the 5 good years that I have had with my family.
