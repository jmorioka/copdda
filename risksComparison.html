Risks of Medical Treatment vs. Lung Transplant

Risks of Medical Treatment Risks of Lung Transplant
It doesn't work for everyone. You might not be a match for available donor lungs. Even if you are eligible to get a transplant, you might be unlucky and never get lungs that are a match.
There will still be times when you are short of breath. You have to take a lot of drugs (dozens per day), and some of them will make you sick or at risk of being sick in other ways.
You’ll still need to use oxygen and inhalers. The transplant process is stressful for you and even more stressful for your caregiver.
Your independence will still be limited. Complications from the transplant could make you have to stay in the hospital for months or undergo painful medical treatments.
After the transplant you have to visit the transplant doctors regularlyand you may have to live close to the hospital.
Lots of little things change: there’ll be foods you can’t eat, places you shouldn’t go, and things can’t do.
Transplant is expensive.
