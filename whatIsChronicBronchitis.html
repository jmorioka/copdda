What is chronic bronchitis?

Video Transcript

Like the last video explained, COPD is one word for two diseases. The first disease we’ll talk about is “chronic bronchitis.”

Bronchial tubes are tubes that branch off of your windpipe and let air travel from your windpipe into your lungs.

Bronchitis is the name for what happens when those tubes become swollen. This swelling can also be called “inflammation.” That inflammation leads to the build up of mucus in the tube, which blocks them and makes it harder to breathe. People with chronic bronchitis also cough a lot, and cough up mucus. This coughing gets worse in cold and damp weather.
